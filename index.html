The Day of the dead, a celebration of life!

The days of the dead are truly a celebration of life. When children dance with caricatures of death, eat skull sugar and learn to respect that life is brief, they learn there is a circle to life and to not fear death.

Celebrating The Day of the Dead has a long history since prehispanic times in Mexican Tradition.

The Day of the dead is celebrated on:
◽October 31 is the day of souls of limbo, those who were never born or did not receive baptism.
◽November 1st is the day of the children and All Saints Day.
◽November 2nd is All Souls Day or the Day of the Dead.
